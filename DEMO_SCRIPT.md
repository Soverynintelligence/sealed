# Demo narration (read aloud while you record)

Target ~2:30. Speak at a normal pace — don't rush. Have two MetaMask accounts
ready (Account 1 = you/contractor, Account 2 = the customer), both with a little
testnet MON. Use a real-feeling quote on screen: customer **Pat**, scope
**"Pond rebuild — 24×18×3, all boulder"**, total **26564** — so it matches the words below.

---

**[Problem — screen on the app's home]**
> "Hey, I'm Jon. I build ponds for a living, and I run a small AI company on the side. This is Sealed — it fixes a problem I actually have. Every week I hand out quotes: a price, a scope, a handshake. But there's no real record of what we agreed to. If a customer later says the number was different, I've got a screenshot at best. So I put the agreement itself on the blockchain."

**[Seal — fill the form, click Seal, confirm in MetaMask, then open the tx]**
> "Here's a quote. Customer, the scope of the job, and the total — twenty-six thousand five hundred for a pond rebuild. I hit Seal, and my wallet asks me to confirm. Done — that quote is now locked onchain. Only a hash of the details goes up, so the private stuff stays private, but the agreement is permanent. And if I pull up the transaction… there it is, live on Monad. Real — not a screenshot."

**[Send + verify — copy link, open in new tab as the customer]**
> "Now I copy the link and send it to my customer. When they open it, the app reads the quote straight off the chain — who sealed it, the amount, the date. And if anyone messes with that link — changes the price — the app catches it and won't let them accept. It has to match what's onchain."

**[Accept — switch MetaMask to Account 2, click Accept, confirm]**
> "So my customer reviews it, connects their own wallet, and hits Accept. Their wallet confirms — and now it's sealed on both sides. My wallet, their wallet, both timestamps. And the contract won't even let me accept my own quote — acceptance has to come from the customer. That's what makes it real proof."

**[Close — screen on the ✓ accepted state, or click View onchain]**
> "That's it. Now neither of us can rewrite it — the price, the scope, the date, the acceptance — all locked on Monad. No more he-said-she-said. That's Sealed."

---

Tips: pause the recording between steps if you need to line up MetaMask; you can
trim later, or just do it in one take. If a transaction is slow to confirm, cut
that dead air out. Keep the whole thing under 3:00 (hard cap).
